Chicago SRW
CBOT  ·  ZW

Eastern Corn Belt to the Mid-Atlantic. The speculative benchmark — soft, low-protein, cookie and cracker class.

When managed money moves in or out of wheat, it moves here first. Calendar spreads reveal carry structure and old-crop tightness.

↓
Kansas City HRW
KCBT  ·  KE

Great Plains — Kansas, Oklahoma, Texas. The largest U.S. wheat class. Bread flour. Drought-tolerant, export-critical.

Less speculative noise than CBOT, more tied to export fundamentals. Watch HRW spreads when Plains crop condition shifts.

↓
Minneapolis HRS
MGEX  ·  MWE

Northern Plains — North Dakota, Minnesota, Montana. Spring-planted. Premium protein. The blending class.

Most weather-sensitive contract. When HRS firms sharply against HRW, protein is scarce and end-users are paying for it.

↓
Paris Milling
EuroNext  ·  EBM

France, Germany, Romania. EU soft wheat belt. The contract that competes with U.S. origin for global export business.

When French FOB drops below U.S. Gulf, American wheat loses export sales — full stop. EUR/USD is embedded in every quote.

Cross-class  ·  Ratios

SRW vs. HRW vs. HRS. Wheat vs. corn. Beans vs. corn. The relationships between classes and competing crops.

Class spread compression or widening signals structural shifts in the supply picture before flat price reflects them.
