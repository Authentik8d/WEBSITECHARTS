Chicago SRW — Calendar Spreads
CBOT  ·  ZW  ·  Soft Red Winter  ·  Weekly

Eastern Corn Belt to the Mid-Atlantic. Low protein, high starch. The speculative benchmark — where managed money enters and exits the wheat market first. Calendar spreads here reveal old-crop carry structure and pre-harvest supply tension before flat price reacts.

  • Mar / May — Near-term carry barometer; nearby premium signals tight old-crop ownership.
  • May / Jul — Pre-harvest spread; widens when old-crop is expected tight through harvest.
  • Jul / Sep — Harvest pressure; full carry here means bins are filling on schedule.
  • Sep / Dec — Post-harvest commercial storage signal; full carry = well-supplied, bearish lean.
  • Dec / Mar+1 — Winter carry; breaks from normal structure when late-season balance sheet shifts.
Kansas City HRW — Calendar Spreads
KCBT  ·  KE  ·  Hard Red Winter  ·  Weekly

Great Plains — Kansas, Oklahoma, Texas, Nebraska. The largest U.S. wheat class by volume. Eleven to thirteen percent protein: the bread flour standard. Less speculative traffic than CBOT; HRW spreads track export fundamentals and Plains crop condition more directly than managed-money flow.

  • Mar / May — Near-term carry; inversion here means Plains country elevators are reluctant to deliver.
  • May / Jul — HRW pre-harvest; a widening spread in spring signals tighter-than-expected old-crop.
  • Jul / Sep — Harvest-to-fall transition; gauges how quickly Great Plains grain moves to export position.
  • Sep / Dec — HRW fall carry; tracks commercial willingness to store after harvest movement slows.
  • Dec / Mar+1 — Winter carry; reflects export pace and whether Plains stocks are drawing faster than expected.
Minneapolis HRS — Calendar Spreads
MGEX  ·  MWE  ·  Hard Red Spring  ·  Weekly

Northern Plains — North Dakota, Minnesota, Montana. Spring-planted, fall-harvested. Thirteen to fifteen percent protein: the blending class that millers reach for when they need to lift a flour batch. The most weather-sensitive contract in the complex — a wet spring in the Red River Valley moves HRS spreads faster than any WASDE revision.

  • Mar / May — Near-term carry during planting season; inversion warns of tighter spring protein supply.
  • May / Jul — HRS pre-harvest; widening in June signals growing season stress before crop reports confirm it.
  • Jul / Sep — Harvest-to-fall; reveals how quickly Northern Plains bins fill after the combine runs.
  • Sep / Dec — HRS fall carry; millers and blenders signal demand urgency through this spread.
  • Dec / Mar+1 — Winter carry; tightness here often means protein-scarce conditions will persist into spring.
Paris Milling Wheat — Calendar Spreads
EuroNext  ·  EBM  ·  EU Soft Milling Wheat  ·  Weekly

France, Germany, Romania — the EU soft wheat belt. The contract that competes directly with U.S. and Black Sea origin for global export business. Priced in euros per tonne, so the EUR/USD cross is embedded in every quote. FWH spreads track the competitive pulse of the Atlantic basin export market.

  • May / Sep — Bridges the EU harvest window; reveals whether French traders expect a comfortable supply transition.
  • Sep / Dec — European fall carry; tracks post-harvest export pace through Black Sea and French ports.
  • Dec / Mar+1 — EU winter carry; tightness signals that European supply is drawing faster than seasonal norms.
  • May+1 / Sep+1 — Deferred year carry; signals how far forward the market is pricing European supply normalcy.
Inter-Market Spreads & Ratios
Cross-class  ·  Cross-commodity  ·  Monthly & Weekly

The relationships between wheat classes and competing crops that flat price alone cannot reveal. Class spreads signal structural shifts in the supply picture — often before individual contracts react. Wheat-to-grain ratios track feed vs. food competition and tell you whether wheat is gaining or losing ground as a feed ingredient.

  • SRW − HRW — The primary inter-class spread; compression signals SRW oversupply or HRW tightness.
  • SRW − HRS — Soft vs. premium protein; widens when protein scarcity bids up the spring class.
  • HRS − HRW — Protein premium spread; the clearest signal of milling demand for high-protein blending wheat.
  • Wheat / Corn Ratio — When ratio falls below 1.2, wheat competes as feed; below 1.0, feedlots replace corn with wheat.
  • Beans / Corn Ratio — Planting intention proxy; tracks the acres competition that shapes corn supply and indirectly, wheat carry.
